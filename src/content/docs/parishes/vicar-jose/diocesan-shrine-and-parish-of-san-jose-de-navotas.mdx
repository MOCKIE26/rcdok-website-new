It is devoted to Saint Joseph, the patron saint of laborers and the foster father of Jesus Christ. For the neighborhood, the parish acts as a hub for culture and spirituality. Its status as a shrine emphasizes its significance for religious ceremonies, pilgrimages, and devotion. Every day, the church holds religious education programs, confession services, and Masses. It regularly participates in outreach programs to help the underprivileged and disenfranchised in the community. To comfortably house parishioners, the construction combines modern elements with traditional Filipino church architecture. Festivals are devoutly observed, particularly the feast of Saint Joseph.

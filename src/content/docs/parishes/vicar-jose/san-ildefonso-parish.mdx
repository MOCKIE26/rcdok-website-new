It is dedicated to Saint Ildefonso, an archbishop from Toledo in the 7th century, who was well-known for his devotion to the Virgin Mary. The parish’s rich history reflects the local community’s cultural legacy. It regularly hosts religious events that involve parishioners of all ages, such as masses and novenas. Traditional Filipino and Spanish colonial elements are combined in the church’s architecture. It acts as a hub for social outreach initiatives, catechism, and spiritual development. To promote communal cohesion, the parish actively participates in regional festivals and celebrations. Its continuous initiatives aim to enhance its members’ moral principles, faith, and altruism.

Originally part of Our Lady of Grace and later Sts. Peter and John Parish, the Sacred Heart of Jesus community in Morning Breeze evolved from a 1963 chapel served by Oblate Fathers into a fully independent parish established by Cardinal Jaime Sin in 1984. Following decades of fervent prayer, the community constructed an imposing new church dedicated in 1990, which subsequently underwent extensive renovations and expansions under the leadership of priests like Fr. Francisco Bagadiong and Fr. Rufino “Gigi” Yabut. These improvements introduced vital facilities such as the Sacred Heart of Jesus Pastoral Center, a medical clinic, and an ossuary, all while shifting the pastoral focus toward building a vibrant “living Church” through home enthronements, street masses, and active lay involvement. Today, the parish stands as a spiritual haven and a center for social services, dedicated to strengthening the faith-life of the Morning Breeze community through a deep devotion to the Sacred Heart.
